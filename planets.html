Planets

Kepler Observatory

Scientists have been on the lookout for potentially habitable planets for if we ever had to leave Earth. NASA is currently using the Kepler Space Observatory to locate Earth-like planets in the event that Earth became uninhabitable. So far, Kepler has discovered 1,284 planets. These include Kepler 452b, Kepler 186f, and Kepler 22b. All of these planets could potentially sustain human life and possibly alien life.

Hubble Telescope

The Hubble Telescope is a space telescope launched by NASA on April 24, 1990. It is approximately 347 miles above Earth, and it remains in low orbit. The Hubble has made many major discoveries. The Hubble Telescope determined that the universe is 14 billion years old, and that the universe is expanding at 157320 mph, or 43.7 miles per second. The Hubble has also been able to locate black holes, and that the there are approximately one septillion planets in the universe (10^24 or 1 followed by 24 zeros).
